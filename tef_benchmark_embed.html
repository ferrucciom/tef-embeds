Editorial risks — what TEF can learn
Key editorial risk Self-promotion creep: resisting the pull to use content to promote TEF programmes is the hardest discipline to maintain Thesis drift: expanding beyond the original lens risks losing the audience that came for exactly that Voice dilution at scale: as more partners and formats are added, the sharpness of the original editorial question softens Data dependency: authority rests entirely on original data — without it, reports become commentary indistinguishable from competitors Delegation risk: handing content to a hired journalist without preserving the original voice diluted what made Lux distinctive post-2023 Without a clear institutional editorial thesis, no compounding identity builds over time — individual voice fills the gap but cannot fully substitute Single-person dependency: if Azhar stops, Exponential View stops Individual departure: Clifford stepping back from EF CEO left a visible gap the institution hadn't built around

The European landscape — active but not equivalent
The most serious voices in Europe's deep tech space operate in different registers. Sifted covers European deep tech with genuine editorial rigour, but as journalism — news-driven, from the outside. Dealroom produces a wide range of data reports across the European ecosystem, including on deep tech, sometimes in partnership with other players — rigorous and widely cited, but without editorial narrative. Several European VC funds — notably Speedinvest, Balderton, and Atomico — publish with content ambitions, but their output is either portfolio-adjacent, generalist, or data-driven rather than editorially led.

None of these combine science commercialisation, venture creation, and cultural change under a consistent editorial identity.
